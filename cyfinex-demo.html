Key Findings
01
Insurance gap of $15M identified. Current policy covers $50M against a modelled annual breach exposure of $65M. The board should consider increasing coverage or accepting the residual risk formally.
02
Spend-to-exposure ratio is below industry benchmark. At 12.6%, Nexus is underspending relative to peers in financial services (benchmark 14–18%). A $400k–$800k increase in targeted controls would close this gap.
03
Legacy SIEM delivering poor ROI. At 1.8x risk reduction per dollar, the Legacy SIEM represents $148k in annual waste. Replacement with a modern SIEM/SOAR stack is recommended in H2.
04
Ransomware remains the primary financial threat. At 18% annual probability and $28.4M expected loss, ransomware represents 44% of total exposure. Current EDR investment in CrowdStrike is the highest-ROI control at 8.8x.
05
Unpatched authentication vulnerability (CVE-2024-4121) requires board attention. This critical finding carries $12.4M potential impact and has been open for 6 days. Remediation is in progress with estimated closure in 48 hours.
Recommended Actions
→
Increase insurance coverage to $75M to close current gap and provide headroom for tail risk scenarios (est. premium uplift: ~$120k/year).
→
Reallocate $148k from Legacy SIEM to enhanced endpoint detection or identity security tooling — estimated risk reduction uplift of $860k ALE.
→
Commission third-party vendor access review to close the supply chain exposure event currently flagged at $6.1M potential impact.
